📽️ Physics Essentials — Content Videos

As part of the broader course development effort for EPPHYS152 at the University of Newcastle, I designed, scripted, and filmed a suite of short (2–5 minute) explainer videos. Each video targets a single concept and was built to slot directly into the Canvas course structure, so students encounter it at exactly the right moment in their learning journey.

⚙️ Mechanics 🌀 Forces ⚡ Energy 〰️ Waves
2–5 Minutes per video
4 Topic areas
100% Scripted & filmed by me

The videos were made with a bridging student in mind — someone stepping into university for the first time who needs concepts made clear, intuitive, and approachable before the mathematical machinery kicks in. Each script was drafted to prioritise physical intuition first, with worked examples and real-world context woven throughout.

As these videos were produced for and are the property of the University of Newcastle, they live within the course's Canvas environment. One example is showcased below — additional videos are available on request.

My Production Process

Each video went through a deliberate pipeline to ensure the final result was both pedagogically sound and genuinely engaging to watch.

  • 1
    Concept Mapping

    I identified the concepts students find most challenging in each topic area, drawing on my experience as a tutor and lab demonstrator, to decide which ideas most deserved their own dedicated video.

  • 2
    Scripting

    Every video was fully scripted before filming. Scripts follow an explicit pedagogical arc: hook the student with a real-world question, build intuition, introduce formalism, then consolidate with a worked example.

  • 3
    Visual Planning

    Alongside each script I planned the visual elements — diagrams, on-screen worked examples, and any demonstrations — so the visuals and narration were fully synchronised from the outset.

  • 4
    Filming & Recording

    I filmed and recorded each video, with the help of the content team at the University of Newcastle. We managed to get through 40+ videos in just a few hours!

  • 5
    Editing & Integration

    After editing, videos were formatted for upload and embedded directly into Canvas at the appropriate module, alongside the course notes and assessment materials I developed.

Featured Example

Below is an example video from the course — Video 2: Significant Figures — The Key to Precise Physics. Significant figures are one of those foundational skills students often underestimate and subsequently struggle with throughout their degree, so getting this explanation right early matters a great deal.

✦ Example Video

Significant Figures — The Key to Precise Physics

EPPHYS152 · Video 2 · University of Newcastle · 2025

This video introduces significant figures as a fundamental tool for communicating precision in physics. It walks students through the rules for identifying and applying significant figures, why they matter in scientific measurement, and how to use them correctly in calculations — all before they encounter them in a formal lab or assessment context.
