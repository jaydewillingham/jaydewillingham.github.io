Outreach and Extracurriculars

Children’s University Educator

Children's University, 2023 - Present
I presented 30 minute sessions to ~10-50 primary school students, with the aim to get them interested and excited about science and particularly physics. You can see a image of me and our "air cannon" demonstration below.

ExperimentFest Educator

University of Newcastle, 2024
Not all highschools have the facilities to demonstrate the experiments that are discussed in the HSC. Therefore, the University of Newcastle holds ExperimentFest each year to guide the students through key experiments, the related physics and help them reach a deeper understanding. I was fortunate to help demonstrate experiments, that demonstrated the nature of light, to groups of ~10 students at a time.

HunterWISE Women in STEM Mentor

University of Newcastle, 2022
This opportunity involved visiting a group of year 8 high school students that identified as female each week over eight weeks to guide them through a project or experiment of their design. This culminated in a poster and presentation at a conference, held at the University. The aim is to help an underrepresented group in STEM to have a first hand experience with science and introduce them to a range of successful and emerging women in scientific careers.

University Club Founder

University of Newcastle, 2020-2023
At the end of my first year of undergrad I created the club "UoN Musos", a club that aimed to bring together students interested in making and talking about music. I became president for two years of my undergrad and during this time ran a number of succesful events and directed/produced our first musical which was extremely succesful. I then became treasurer in my final year to transition out of club leadership. Under this role I acted as vocal director in our second musical and help secured substantial university funding for future productions.

Research Experience

CSIRO Studentship

Supervisor: Dr Maxim Voronkov
CSIRO, November 2024 - February 2025

We investigated the hyperfine structures of methanol masers in observational radio data. Our work included simulating these structures, modeling the data, and applying statistical analyses to better understand these poorly characterized features and their potential implications.

Bachelor of Philosophy Research

Supervisor: Professor Andrew Hopkins
Macquarie University, 2024

This study quantifies uncertainties, evaluates the robustness of star formation rate (SFR) calculations, and compares their performance to model-derived SFRs, highlighting areas for improvement within the field.

Bachelor of Philosophy Research

Supervisor: Dr Tayyaba Zafar
Macquarie University, 2024

We explored the viability of using observational radio fluxes from star-forming galaxies to constrain spectral energy distribution models, enabling improved parameter estimation.
